How to Prepare for an Earthquake

Earthquakes can strike without warning, but preparation can significantly reduce injuries and save lives. This guide explains what to do before, during, and after an earthquake so individuals and families can stay safe and ready.

Safety & Awareness

Before an Earthquake

Take these steps to prepare your home and family in advance.

  • Identify safe spots such as under sturdy tables.
  • Secure bookshelves, appliances, and heavy objects.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, and supplies.
  • Plan evacuation routes and meeting points.

During an Earthquake

Stay calm and protect yourself from falling objects.

  • Drop to your hands and knees.
  • Cover your head and neck.
  • Hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay indoors unless the building is unsafe.

After an Earthquake

Be cautious and aware of ongoing dangers.

  • Check yourself and others for injuries.
  • Beware of aftershocks.
  • Avoid damaged buildings and power lines.
  • Listen for emergency updates.

Why Preparation Matters

Preparation plays a big role in staying safe during an earthquake. When people know what to do, they react faster and avoid panic. Getting ready helps reduce injuries, protects families, and keeps the community stronger. Simple steps like preparing a safety kit, securing furniture, and learning safe spots can make a big difference when the shaking starts.

And Remember

Always stay informed and review your emergency plan regularly. Practicing earthquake drills and teaching others what to do helps everyone stay calm. A few minutes of preparation today can save lives in the future. Safety begins with awareness — and every small step matters.
