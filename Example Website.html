Why here, and where is risk highest?

Turkey sits at the collision of the Eurasian, African, and Arabian plates. The North Anatolian Fault runs roughly east‚Äìwest across northern Turkey, and the East Anatolian Fault cuts NE‚ÄìSW in the southeast. These boundaries store strain that releases as earthquakes.

How buildings fail and ways to make them safer

In Turkey's most devastating earthquakes, many buildings have collapsed due to preventable design flaws. This section breaks down why so many structures failed and the engineering solutions that could've prevented thousands of them from collapsing.

Common failure modes

  • Soft-story collapse (weak ground floor with open shops/parking)
  • Poor concrete and rebar detailing
  • Irregular additions and heavy rooftop structures

Safety measures

  • Follow post-1999 & post-2018 building codes
  • Retrofit: add shear walls, brace frames, improve connections
  • Non-structural safety: anchor furniture, secure water heaters
